RumourLens
Early Rumour Detection with Multi-View Explanations
I. Introduction
Hundreds of thousands of rumours emerge every day. Algorithmic models shall therefore support users of social platforms and provide alerts to prevent users from accidentally spreading rumours. However, existing alerting mechanisms fail to fully incorporate the dynamic nature of social platforms, i.e., they assume that rumours can be characterized using a fixed set of entities along with predefined propagation patterns, and focus on post-mortem explanations after a rumour has been classified. This paper presents RumourLens, a system that enables immediate monitoring and tracking of potential rumours as they emerge. To this end, it identifies local anomalies related to individual entities, as well as global anomalies on the level of subgraphs of a network of entities. RumourLens provides various views on such local and global anomalies, thereby providing detailed explanations on emerging rumours and supporting their critical exploration.
II. Contributions
1. Local anomaly indicators: RumourLens features an efficient statistical model to reason on the anomalousness of entities and their relations. This model assesses the evolution of entities and their relations over time, as well as their similarity with other entities and relations.
2. Global anomaly indicators: RumourLens features a subgraph search algorithm based on scan statistics to identify anomalous structures in a social network.
3. Early rumour detection: Based on the anomaly indicators, RumourLens captures early signals of potential rumours and flags them for further assessment.
4. Multi-view explanations for rumour exploration: RumourLens provides a wide range of explanation measures, including those based on features, general statistics, and graph properties. As such, it enables users to explore the characteristics of the detected rumours.
